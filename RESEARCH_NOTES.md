# Research Notes: Wedge Strategy

AdminAvenger's public wedge is now **Money Back Avenger**.

The first completed proof vertical remains **Train Delay / Delay Repay**. It is useful because it proves the product can turn a messy message into evidence, missing information, confidence, a draft, and chase steps. It is not the strongest launch wedge because train delay refunds are narrower, intermittent, and partly handled by existing rail/operator systems.

The next validation candidate is **broadband/mobile price-rise letters**. These are a strong fit because they arrive as emails, messages, or letters; involve real money; require explanation; and can be confusing because UK telecom price-rise rules have changed recently. Users can paste a provider notice and test whether AdminAvenger helps them understand what changed, what evidence is missing, and what to do next.

**Subscriptions and unwanted renewals** are a strong second candidate because they are common, easy to understand, and tied directly to saving money. They should be validated after the price-rise workflow rather than built in parallel.

**Missing delivery, faulty goods, and returns** remain important because users often have seller or courier messages, order references, missing proof, and a clear desired outcome: refund, replacement, or escalation.

**Energy, water, benefits, financial complaints, and other regulated/high-stakes areas** should come later. They need more trust, clearer caveats, stronger evidence handling, and probably professional/legal review before being treated as launch workflows.

Do not build five verticals at once.

Verticals are earned from real user behaviour, not declared from architecture. The product can support multiple demos, but the launch wedge must be one focused workflow.
