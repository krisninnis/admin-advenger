# Community Helper Pack Core v1

Status: implemented (standalone data/model/builder layer only). No UI, no
HomeView routing, no automatic detection, no changes to OCR, document
intake, file intake, photo intake, or the main decision-engine classifier.
This is the first implementation layer of the approved plan in
`docs/product/community-helper-pack-v1-plan.md`.

## Why this exists

Carers, support workers, occupational therapists, housing officers, family
members, and other trusted helpers often need to explain how an admin
problem is affecting someone's daily life to another human - a support
worker, OT, adviser, housing officer, social worker, clinician, or trusted
person. Community Helper Pack Core v1 adds a safe, deterministic way to turn
a short description of a situation into a structured pack of facts,
questions, and safe next steps for that human to review.

Standing principle: **AdminAvenger helps prepare. You stay in control.**
This feature stays preparation-only. It does not decide safeguarding,
capacity, diagnosis, care eligibility, or entitlement, and it never sends,
submits, or contacts anyone automatically.

## What was added

1. **`src/lib/communityHelperPack.ts`** (new) - the model and builder:
   - `CommunityHelperSituationType`: 11 values covering missed letters/
     deadlines, difficulty understanding letters, housing repair/access
     difficulty, OT/support visit preparation, carer organising letters,
     support worker meeting notes, daily routine admin overwhelm,
     communication difficulty, vulnerability/financial admin concern, an
     urgent safeguarding-like signpost, and a conservative unknown fallback.
   - `CommunityHelperPack`: 15 fields (situationType, title, summary, daily
     life impact, admin barriers, communication barriers, key facts to
     check, evidence to gather, questions to ask, cannotKnow, safe next
     steps, preparation-only notes, consent/control notes, risk warnings,
     signposting).
   - `buildCommunityHelperPack(input)`: the main export. Deterministic and
     keyword/regex based - no cloud AI, no network calls, no randomness.
   - `flattenCommunityHelperPackText(pack)`: joins every field into one
     string for safety scanning, mirroring `workplaceSupportPack.ts`'s
     equivalent helper.
2. **Cautious keyword-based classification inside the builder only**
   (`detectCommunityHelperSituationType`). This never touches
   `src/lib/decisionEngine/` or its classifier - it is a private, separate
   cascade of regex checks, following the exact architectural pattern
   `workplaceSupportPack.ts` already established for workplace situations.
   Safety-critical situations (urgent safeguarding-like wording, then
   vulnerability/financial concern) are always checked first, ahead of more
   specific-but-lower-stakes situations, ahead of generic ones.
3. **Consent and helper-control safety wording** (`buildConsentAndControlNotes`):
   - When helping someone else (either an explicit `role: "helping_someone"`
     or detected from language like "I'm a carer for my mum" or "helping my
     dad"), the pack tells the helper to check the person is happy for the
     pack to exist, to keep them involved, to avoid unnecessary detail, that
     the pack is not a secret record, and to check wording with them before
     sharing.
   - When `role: "supporting_people_at_work"` (or detected professional
     language), the pack instead references following the helper's own
     organisation's rules, avoiding unnecessary sensitive detail, and
     involving the person supported.
   - Otherwise (`for_myself`/default), a lighter note: "You control what you
     save, download, share, or delete."
   - Never used, anywhere: "act on their behalf", "they lack capacity",
     "they cannot decide", "you can take control" - these are asserted
     absent in tests and are simply never generated by any code path.
4. **Urgent safeguarding-like signposting**: a dedicated
   `urgent_safeguarding_like_signpost` situation type, triggered by cautious
   phrase matches for immediate danger, abuse, neglect, exploitation,
   coercion, or being unable to care for oneself. Its `riskWarnings` and
   `safeNextSteps` always include the exact required sentence:

   > If someone may be in immediate danger, contact emergency services or
   > the relevant local safeguarding service. AdminAvenger cannot decide
   > safeguarding concerns.

   The pack never claims to confirm, decide, or prove a safeguarding issue.
5. **Financial admin concern safety**: a dedicated
   `vulnerability_financial_admin_concern` situation type for language like
   someone else controlling money, a missing bank card, or being pressured
   to hand over money. The pack only ever gathers facts and suggests
   speaking to an independent adviser, the person's bank, or a social
   worker - it never claims financial abuse is proven, never counts money as
   owed/recovered/saved, and its `cannotKnow` list explicitly states that
   whether abuse or wrongdoing occurred cannot be known from this pack
   alone.
6. **Draft/checklist boundaries**: the `CommunityHelperPack` type only ever
   contains checklist/next-step style string arrays (facts, evidence,
   questions, next steps, notes). There is no `draftMessage`, `autoSend`,
   `sendTo`, or `emailBody` field anywhere in the type or its builder - this
   is asserted directly in the test suite (see below).
7. **10 synthetic golden fixtures** added to `src/lib/goldenLetters.ts`
   under a new `"community_helper"` category (`GoldenLetterCategory`), one
   per situation type (excluding the unknown fallback). Every fixture:
   - Is entirely synthetic - no real names, no real addresses, no real
     phone numbers, no real medical record numbers, no real personal data.
     **These are not Kristian's real PIP documents.**
   - Is run only through the existing, unmodified main classifier
     (`analyseDecisionProblem`), exactly like the existing `"workplace"`
     fixtures - none of these fixtures' text matches any existing
     parking/bailiff/TV Licence/bank/council tax/debt/consumer/benefits
     keyword pattern, so every one of them routes to the conservative
     `unknown_admin_dispute` fallback (verified against
     `src/lib/decisionEngine/classifier.ts`'s pattern lists before writing
     the fixtures). The Community Helper Pack builder classifies and
     handles each situation separately, on its own.
8. **`src/lib/__tests__/communityHelperPack.test.ts`** (new) - 19 tests
   covering: situation-type detection for all 10 real types plus the
   conservative unknown fallback, urgent-safeguarding-over-financial-concern
   priority, the exact required emergency signposting sentence, that
   safeguarding/financial-abuse claims are never made, consent/control notes
   for all three roles (including auto-detection without an explicit role),
   that banned helper-control phrases never appear, that the pack type never
   exposes a sendable draft/email field, that the standing "AdminAvenger
   helps prepare. You stay in control." principle is always present, date/
   reference extraction into key facts, and determinism.
9. **`src/lib/safetyWording.ts`** - added a new `community_helper_claim`
   forbidden-phrase group (see "Safety wording implementation note" below)
   and a `collectTextFromCommunityHelperPack` collector for future use.
10. **`src/lib/__tests__/safetyWordingRegression.test.ts`** - added one test
    asserting the new `community_helper_claim` phrases are detected
    correctly, alongside the existing outcome/money/automation/overclaim
    tests. No combined-artifact regression block was added for Community
    Helper Pack (unlike the existing "Workplace Support Pack ResultViewModel
    output..." block), because Core v1 deliberately does not integrate with
    `ResultViewModel`/`AdviserExportPack`/`CaseProgress` yet.
11. **`src/lib/__tests__/goldenLetterCorpus.test.ts`** - added an assertion
    that the `"workplace"` and `"community_helper"` categories are present,
    alongside the existing category checks.

## Safety wording implementation note

The task brief listed a forbidden-wording set that included the bare word
"diagnosis". This was deliberately **not** added as a bare, single-word
global ban. Every other entry in every existing forbidden-phrase group
(`FORBIDDEN_OUTCOME_CLAIMS`, `FORBIDDEN_ADVICE_CLAIMS`, etc.) is a
multi-word **claim phrase** ("you are entitled", "money saved"), never a
single dictionary word - banning bare "diagnosis" would also have caught the
plan's own required, safe `cannotKnow` wording ("whether a diagnosis...
applies") and existing, legitimate decision-engine copy elsewhere in the app
(`wcaLcwra.ts`: "not just your diagnosis"). Instead:

- `FORBIDDEN_COMMUNITY_HELPER_CLAIMS` (group `community_helper_claim`) bans
  the claim-shaped phrases: "you are diagnosed", "this proves disability",
  "this proves neglect", "safeguarding issue confirmed", "risk score", "care
  score", "eligibility score", "they qualify", "council must provide",
  "needs this equipment", "needs this adaptation", "cannot live alone",
  "lacks capacity", "financial abuse proven", "money owed", "contacted
  automatically".
- The Community Helper Pack builder's own generated `cannotKnow` copy uses
  the phrase "whether a diagnosis or health condition applies" - the safe,
  hedged form the plan requires - which does not collide with any forbidden
  phrase above.
- "money saved"/"money recovered"/"submitted automatically" style
  automation/money overclaims were already covered by the existing
  `FORBIDDEN_MONEY_CLAIMS`/`FORBIDDEN_AUTOMATION_CLAIMS` groups and were not
  duplicated.

## Consent, helper-control, safeguarding, and financial-concern behaviour

See "What was added" items 3-5 above for the full behaviour. In short: the
pack always tells the helper they stay in control of what is saved, shared,
or deleted; when helping someone else it adds explicit checks about consent
and involvement; safeguarding-like input always signposts emergency
services/the local safeguarding service and never claims to decide the
issue itself; financial-concern input always gathers facts only and never
claims abuse is proven or money is owed/recovered.

## What this is not (yet)

- No UI. Nothing in `src/views/` or `src/components/` references
  `communityHelperPack.ts`.
- No HomeView routing. `buildCommunityHelperPack` is not called from
  anywhere in the app's existing input flow.
- No automatic detection. Nothing scans a pasted "Check a message" input and
  silently decides to build a Community Helper Pack - this only happens
  when a future caller explicitly invokes `buildCommunityHelperPack`.
- No changes to OCR, document/file/photo intake, or
  `src/lib/decisionEngine/` (classifier or modules).
- No integration with `ResultViewModel`, `AdviserExportPack`, or
  `CaseProgress` - those are explicitly future branches (for example
  `community-helper-resultviewmodel-v1`,
  `community-helper-case-progress-v1`,
  `community-helper-adviser-export-v1`), following the same integration
  path `workplaceSupportPack.ts` already went through.
