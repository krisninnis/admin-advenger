# Pilot Test Script v1

## Purpose

Use this script for a small, trusted AdminAvenger pilot with 3-5 people.

This is not a public launch. The goal is to learn whether testers understand the preparation-only model, whether the wording feels safe, and whether the current flows help people organise stressful life admin without implying advice, decisions, or automatic action.

## Who To Invite

Invite 3-5 trusted testers only.

Good fit:

- carers
- family helpers
- people who regularly deal with letters, bills, forms, or admin messages
- people who understand life-admin stress
- trusted supporters who can describe confusion points clearly

Avoid for this first pilot:

- public social media testers
- people in an urgent live crisis
- people expecting legal, benefits, care, medical, debt, housing, employment, or safeguarding advice
- people who may paste highly sensitive real information without understanding the local-first boundary

## What To Say Before The Test

Use plain wording before the tester touches the app:

AdminAvenger helps prepare. You stay in control.

It can help explain what a message or letter appears to be, gather key details, prepare editable drafts or checklists, and organise what to check next.

It does not give legal, benefits, care, medical, or safeguarding advice.

It does not decide for you.

It does not send anything automatically.

It does not submit anything automatically.

It does not contact anyone automatically.

For this first pilot, please do not enter real highly sensitive details. Use the synthetic examples, or lightly anonymise anything you choose to paste. Remove names, addresses, reference numbers, account numbers, passwords, medical details, and anything you are not comfortable testing with.

If something is urgent or someone may be unsafe, do not rely on this app. Contact an appropriate person or service directly.

## Session Setup

Before starting:

- use the production alias intended for the pilot
- start with a clean browser profile or clear local AdminAvenger data
- explain that downloaded files are controlled by the user and their device
- remind the tester that they can stop at any point
- tell the tester you are testing clarity and safety, not testing them

## Task 1: Normal Admin Input Flow

Ask the tester to:

1. Open Home.
2. Use a synthetic or lightly anonymised admin message.
3. Check the message.
4. Read the result.
5. Find what the app says matters.
6. Find what is uncertain or still needs checking.

Questions after Task 1:

- What do you think this tool is doing?
- Did it feel like it was giving advice or just helping prepare?
- Did you think anything was sent automatically?
- What would you copy or share with a trusted person or adviser?
- What was confusing?
- What wording felt reassuring?
- What wording felt risky or too strong?

## Task 2: Community Helper Controlled Public Beta

Ask the tester to:

1. Open the Community Helper controlled public beta route.
2. Read the safety wording.
3. Explain what they think "manual text only" means.
4. Click one example chip.
5. Confirm that the chip only fills the textarea.
6. Paste or edit a short synthetic missed-letter/admin example.
7. Click "Prepare community support notes".
8. Review the result.

Suggested synthetic missed-letter/admin example:

Someone I help has several letters they do not understand. They missed a reply date on one letter and are not sure which documents matter, who to contact, or what to ask next. I want to organise the facts and questions before speaking to someone.

Questions after Task 2:

- What do you think the Community Helper result is trying to do?
- Did the example chip feel like an example, or did it feel like automatic detection?
- Did you understand that you still had to click the prepare button?
- Did the result feel like preparation notes or advice?
- Did anything imply care, safeguarding, diagnosis, capacity, eligibility, equipment, or adaptation decisions?
- What would you copy or share with a trusted person or adviser?
- What was confusing?
- What wording felt reassuring?
- What wording felt risky or too strong?

## Task 3: Community Helper Local Feedback Panel

Ask the tester to:

1. Find the feedback panel after the Community Helper result.
2. Choose useful, partly useful, or not useful.
3. Type one thing that was unclear or missing.
4. Save feedback locally.
5. Clear feedback.

Questions after Task 3:

- Did you understand this feedback stays on this device?
- Did you think the feedback was sent anywhere?
- Did the wording feel optional and local?
- Was the feedback question easy to answer?
- What would you change about the feedback panel?

## Task 4: Copy And Export

Ask the tester to:

1. Find a useful draft, checklist, or notes section.
2. Use a copy action if available.
3. Download an adviser export if available.
4. Open the downloaded Markdown file if comfortable.

Questions after Task 4:

- What would you copy or share?
- Who would you share it with?
- Did the export feel like preparation notes or an official document?
- Did anything imply AdminAvenger had sent or submitted it?
- Did the export include enough uncertainty and caveats?
- Was anything too long or too confusing?

## Task 5: Local Data Control

Ask the tester to:

1. Find Settings.
2. Find the local data control or clear data area.
3. Explain what they think clearing data will do.
4. If using a test browser profile, clear local AdminAvenger data.

Questions after Task 5:

- Did you understand what data might be stored in this browser?
- Did you understand that clearing local data does not delete downloaded files?
- Did you think anything was stored in an account or cloud service?
- Did this make you more or less comfortable testing the app?

## Red Flags

Record a red flag if the tester thinks:

- the app decides eligibility
- the app confirms safeguarding
- the app confirms capacity
- the app confirms diagnosis
- the app decides care needs, equipment, or adaptations
- the app sends, submits, or contacts automatically
- the app gives legal, benefits, care, medical, safeguarding, debt, housing, employment, or financial advice
- "public beta" means safe for real urgent situations
- money is automatically saved, recovered, owed, or counted
- dates are confirmed by the app rather than checked against the source
- it is safe to enter highly sensitive real data without understanding local-first boundaries

If a red flag appears, ask:

- What wording made you think that?
- Where did you see it?
- What would make the boundary clearer?

## Green Flags

Record a green flag if the tester:

- understands preparation-only
- understands they stay in control
- knows nothing is sent automatically
- knows nothing is submitted automatically
- knows no one is contacted automatically
- can explain what they would do next outside the app
- finds copy/export useful
- can identify what is unclear or missing
- understands dates, money, and facts need checking against the original source
- understands Community Helper is manual text only
- understands the feedback panel is local and optional

## Stop/Go Decision

Stop if 2 or more testers misunderstand any of these boundaries:

- advice boundary
- sending/submitting/contacting boundary
- decision/eligibility/safeguarding/capacity/diagnosis boundary
- local-first/privacy boundary

Polish if testers understand the safety model but struggle with wording, layout, length, or navigation.

Continue only if users understand:

- AdminAvenger helps prepare. You stay in control.
- outputs are preparation, not advice
- nothing is sent automatically
- nothing is submitted automatically
- no one is contacted automatically
- humans decide what to do next

## Facilitator Closing Script

Thank you. This pilot is about clarity and safety. AdminAvenger is still a preparation tool. Please do not rely on it for urgent decisions or specialist advice. If you use it again with real information, remove details you are not comfortable testing with and check anything important against the original source or an appropriate person or service.
