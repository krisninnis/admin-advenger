# Trust & Safety Page v1

## Why this page exists

AdminAvenger asks people to paste, upload, or photograph stressful personal admin. A user may be worried, tired, under time pressure, or helping someone else. The product therefore needs a plain-English page that explains what happens, what stays under the user's control, and what AdminAvenger cannot do.

This page is practical. It is not marketing copy, pricing copy, legal terms, or a replacement for the Free-Forever Covenant.

## How it differs from the Covenant page

The Covenant page explains the mission promise: the individual core stays free, safety is not paywalled, and the product must not be funded through exploitative patterns.

The Trust & Safety page explains day-to-day use:

- where the user's letter goes
- what happens in the browser
- how local downloads work
- how dates and money are handled
- what drafts and adviser packs are for
- what AdminAvenger cannot know
- when a user should ask for help

Both pages should use the same public principle:

AdminAvenger helps prepare. You stay in control.

## Required user-facing promises

The page should clearly say:

- AdminAvenger helps users understand what a letter appears to be.
- It helps gather key details.
- It can prepare an editable checklist or adviser pack.
- It does not decide for the user.
- It does not send anything for the user.
- It does not submit anything for the user.
- The user decides what to do next.

## Privacy and local-first wording rules

Use careful wording:

- designed to work locally first
- in this version
- downloaded to your device
- not sent by AdminAvenger
- you control what you enter, save, download, share, or delete

Avoid broad claims that could become inaccurate if future optional integrations are added.

## Safety boundaries

The page must not position AdminAvenger as:

- a legal adviser
- a benefits adviser
- a debt adviser
- a financial adviser
- a medical adviser
- an immigration adviser
- a claims service
- an appeal or submission service
- an eligibility checker
- an outcome predictor

The page should explain that AdminAvenger does not contact anyone for the user and does not replace a qualified adviser.

## Date and money wording rules

Dates:

- may be extracted to help the user notice them
- must be checked against the original letter
- may be misread by OCR or unclear text
- should be verified before action, especially if serious

Money:

- is display-only
- is shown because it appeared in the document or app output
- has not been checked as correct, owed, payable, a saving, or recoverable
- must not be counted as savings or recovery by this page

## Adviser pack wording rules

Adviser packs are preparation records for the user to save, print, or show to someone they trust.

The page should say:

- adviser packs download to the user's device as Markdown
- AdminAvenger does not upload them
- AdminAvenger does not send them anywhere
- the user chooses who sees them
- the user should review them before using or sharing

## What not to claim

Do not claim:

- a certain outcome
- that an organisation has made an incorrect decision
- that a person has a right to a payment or service
- that a demanded amount is correct or incorrect
- that a deadline is final unless the source letter itself clearly says so
- that OCR has read everything perfectly
- that a draft or pack has been sent or filed
- that the app replaces specialist advice
